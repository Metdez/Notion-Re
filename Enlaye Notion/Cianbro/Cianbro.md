# Cianbro (The Cianbro Companies) — GC Intelligence Dossier
*Prepared for Enlaye Business Development · Date of capture: 2026-06-10 · All money in USD millions unless noted*

---

## TL;DR
- **Cianbro is a ~$1.3B-revenue, 100% employee-owned (ESOP) heavy/civil + building contractor headquartered in Pittsfield, ME, structured as ~5 market-sector operating units (Building, Industrial & Manufacturing, Infrastructure, Power & Energy, Modular Manufacturing) PLUS 5 named subsidiary companies (A/Z Corporation, Starcon International, R.C. Stevens, Total Specialty Services, Cianbro Fabrication & Coating) under "The Cianbro Companies" umbrella.** Divisions are organized by *market sector* (not region), with regional offices nested beneath; the subsidiaries are geographically/market specialized.
- **The portfolio is concentrated in alternative-delivery infrastructure (movable bridges, dry docks, marine/seawall) and power/energy (BESS, pumped hydro), with repeat institutional owners (MaineDOT, CTDOT, US Navy/NAVFAC, NPS) and recurring JV partners (The Middlesex Corp., VJS Construction).** Marquee active work: the ~$935M WALK Bridge (CTDOT, Cianbro-Middlesex JV, lift-span float-in ~2030); the $1.7B Portsmouth Naval Shipyard dry-dock mega-project (381 Constructors JV through ~2028); and self-developed $1B Western Maine pumped-storage hydro project.
- **Incumbent tech stack is mature and named: Oracle Primavera P6 (enterprise scheduling), Procore (PM), Touchplan (enterprise Lean), HCSS (heavy-civil), BuildingConnected, BIM/VDC.** Risk/EHS data is strong (OSHA VPP Star Mobile Workforce; only 1 cited OSHA inspection 2020-26, $26,988) but **EMR/TRIR numbers, bonding capacity, and insurance carriers are NOT publicly disclosed** — these are open data gaps.

## Key Findings

### Division count & architecture
Cianbro runs **two overlapping organizational layers**, which is the core mapping mechanic for this account:

1. **Market-sector operating units** inside Cianbro Corporation (the primary internal P&L structure — confirmed by both the site's "What We Do" nav and by press releases that name the owning *market*, e.g., "Cianbro's Industrial & Manufacturing (IDM) market was awarded…"):
   - **Building** · **Industrial & Manufacturing (IDM)** · **Infrastructure** · **Power & Energy** · **Modular Manufacturing** (+ **Support Services**)
2. **Named subsidiary companies** ("family of companies") acquired or spun for specific markets/geographies:
   - **A/Z Corporation** · **Starcon International** · **R.C. Stevens Construction** · **Total Specialty Services** · **Cianbro Fabrication & Coating Corporation** (plus operating LLCs: **Cianbro Equipment, LLC** and **Cianbro Constructors, LLC**).

This means a single project can map to a *market unit* (e.g., Infrastructure) AND be delivered through a *subsidiary* (e.g., A/Z for vertical CM). Cianbro deliberately consolidated its **vertical construction-management group under the A/Z Corporation brand in 2024** to "sell those services more directly" (Andi Vigue, Mainebiz).

### Firmographics
- **Legal/parent:** Cianbro Corporation (Maine corporation, Subchapter S); umbrella "The Cianbro Companies."
- **Founded:** 1949 by the Cianchette Brothers (Carl Cianchette began operations 1946); became Cianbro Corporation 1970; **100% employee-owned since 2003**.
- **Ownership:** ESOP. Per the **National Center for Employee Ownership (NCEO) "Employee Ownership 100"** list, Cianbro is ranked **tied at #48 (3,000 employees), listed as "Cianbro* | Pittsfield | ME | ESOP | 1980 | Construction | 3,000"** — correcting the commonly repeated "30th largest" claim.
- **Revenue trend:** ~$760M (2021) → $1.2B (2022) → The Cianbro Cos. reported **$1,163.0M on ENR's 2023 Top 400 list** → **$1.3B (2025, Mainebiz)**.
- **Employees:** Per Cianbro's official corporate description, **"operating in more than 40 states and employing over 4,000 team members, Cianbro manages and self-performs civil, structural, mechanical, electrical, instrumentation, telecommunications, thermal, fabrication, and coating."** ~12 markets.
- **ENR:** Ranked **No. 96 on the ENR Top 400 Contractors list and ENR New England's 2023 Contractor of the Year** (ENR); listed at company rank #72 on the 2023 Top 400 table (subsidiary-inclusive). ENR self-reported breakdown also cited #14 Top Industrial, #17 Top 150 GC, #28 Top 250 Performer.
- **Federal identifiers (authoritative, USASpending API):** **UEI S175L3H4MKV1 · CAGE 5S864 · DUNS 006854210 · NAICS primary 237990** (Other Heavy & Civil Engineering Construction). SAM-registered since April 2002.
- **Licensed in 31 states + DC** (MWMCA contractor profile).
- **Open-shop / non-union** (a defining cultural attribute since founding); fleet of ~3,500 owned equipment units (Cianbro Equipment, LLC).

### Risk & compliance posture
- **Safety programs:** OSHA **VPP Star Mobile Workforce** (57th US company to join, 2017; the only Star Mobile Workforce participant federally classified in OSHA Region 1 excl. Vermont). Proprietary **"Beyond Zero"** culture program and **CAPP** (Cianbro Accident Prevention Process), behavior-based.
- **OSHA establishment inspections (2020-2026, exact name "Cianbro Corporation"):** 3 total. Only one produced citations — **Insp. #1484351.015 (opened 07/21/2020, I-95 Hermon ME site): 2 violations (reclassified to "Other" on settlement), $26,988 penalty, closed 01/13/2021** (standards 1926.250(a)(1) and 1926.21(b)(2)). A planned inspection at the Tidal Basin DC site (05/22/2025) closed with no violations. **No fatalities/catastrophes** found in the searched window.
- **Certifications:** ISO 9001 compliant; AISC (STD/SBR/CBR); ASME S&U; NBIC R / NBBI (U,S,R,NB); SSPC QP-1 & QP-2; PCI Qualified Erector; Canadian Welding Bureau; CWI/NACE.

## Recommendations

**Stage 1 — Lead with the Infrastructure division + project-controls pain (now).** The WALK Bridge program (~$935M, lift-span float-in 2030) and the $1.7B PNSY dry-dock JV are multi-year, multi-stakeholder, high-risk-exposure jobs running on Primavera P6 + Touchplan, and Norwalk job reqs show project-controls/scheduling headcount actively scaling. This is the single highest-value entry point for risk-management software. **Benchmark to advance:** if Cianbro posts a dedicated risk/insurance, EHS, or VDC/project-controls leadership req (none seen as of this capture), escalate immediately — that is the buying-intent trigger.

**Stage 2 — Map the A/Z vertical-CM consolidation as a second motion.** The 2024 folding of vertical CM under A/Z (with its own CT/MA/NJ/RI office network and design-services arm) created a distinct buyer with its own tools and P&L. Target A/Z separately from the heavy-civil core; its life-sciences/higher-ed/healthcare mix carries different (owner-driven) risk and insurance requirements than the DOT/Navy work.

**Stage 3 — Use the federal/owner concentration as the correlation wedge.** Repeat owners are overwhelmingly public/federal (MaineDOT, CTDOT, Navy/NAVFAC, NPS, GSA) running design-build and CM/GC with prevailing-wage, bonding, and federal-compliance overhead — exactly where risk-management SaaS earns ROI. Frame Enlaye around multi-party JV risk (Middlesex, VJS, Kiewit/TIC recur) and alternative-delivery exposure.

**Decision thresholds that would change the approach:** (a) If Cianbro is found to have standardized on a competing enterprise risk/QA platform beyond P6/Procore/Touchplan, reposition as a complementary risk layer rather than a replacement. (b) If a public EMR/TRIR disclosure surfaces showing rates near/above industry average (construction 2024 TRIR ≈ 2.3), safety/EHS becomes a sharper wedge; their VPP Star status today suggests strong performance, so de-emphasize EHS and emphasize project-controls/financial risk. (c) Watch for a new M&A event (cadence has been ~every 2-3 years) — a fresh acquisition is a high-probability tech-consolidation and procurement-review window.

## Caveats
- **EMR / TRIR / DART numeric values are not publicly disclosed** by Cianbro and are intentionally left null; only the qualitative safety posture (VPP Star, Beyond Zero) and OSHA establishment-inspection records are verifiable.
- **Bonding capacity (single-project/aggregate), surety provider, and insurance carriers/wrap-ups were not found** in public sources — left null.
- **Division-level revenue and employee splits are not disclosed**; only the consolidated ~$1.3B / ~4,000 figures are sourced. The "~5 market units + 5 subsidiaries" count reflects the public site structure and may understate internal regional sub-units.
- **Revenue figures vary by source year and by whether subsidiaries are included** (ENR's "The Cianbro Cos." $1,163.0M vs. Mainebiz "$1.3B in 2025"); third-party aggregators (ZoomInfo $1.3B, RocketReach "$2.5B") are unreliable and were not used for the headline figure.
- **NPS Tidal Basin completion ("Dec 10, 2025, ~$30M under budget, 8 months early") and Cross Town in-service ("late Nov 2025")** are recent and were corroborated via NPS and Canary Media; treat exact obligated dollar totals as cumulative/evolving (USASpending shows obligations rising above announced base values, e.g., Navy N4008524C0001 at $120.3M vs. ~$112.5M announced).
- **Maine SOS charter number, per-project parcel/APN, FEMA flood zones, seismic design categories, exact NTP/substantial-completion dates, and per-state license numbers** could not be sourced at the atomic level and are left null.
- **The $1.7B PNSY mega-project and $935M WALK Bridge are JV/program totals, not Cianbro's sole contract value**; Cianbro's revenue share is undisclosed.
- The **Western Maine pumped-hydro project is in FERC preliminary-permit stage only** (filed Jul 2025) — it is a *self-development*, not yet a construction award, and the permit "does not authorize construction"; the $1B figure and 2033 timeline are developer estimates, not committed contract values.
