# HITT Contracting — GC Intelligence Dossier (Enlaye BD & Competitive Targeting)

## TL;DR
- **HITT Contracting is a privately/family-owned national commercial general contractor with 2025 revenue of $13.1 billion and 3,300+ team members across 16 offices, ranked ENR #10 (debut, 2025 list on 2024 revenue) and ENR #1 Telecommunications/Data Center contractor — making its Mission Critical and Government units the highest-value, highest-risk-exposure BD targets for Enlaye.**
- **HITT runs operations as a matrix: ~16 regional offices (geographic P&L) crossed with ~12 named market-sector units (e.g., Mission Critical, Government, Healthcare, Hospitality, Life Sciences, Advanced Manufacturing) plus two wholly-owned subsidiaries (Brycon Corp., acquired Feb 2025; Central Consulting & Contracting, acquired March 2025). HITT publishes no formal "division count," so 16 regional offices is the firmest atomic number; project→division mapping below uses explicit/geographic/sector evidence.**
- **Incumbent stack: Procore (enterprise PMIS, early adopter, 825+ concurrent projects per Procore), Bluebeam, Autodesk/BIM, OpenSpace reality capture, Hilti Jaibot robotics. Federal IDs: UEI RRKASFUKYMJ4, CAGE 0TDS4, NAICS 236220. Procore incumbency means Enlaye should position as a complementary risk layer, not a PMIS rip-and-replace.**

## Key Findings

**Firmographics.** Legal name HITT Contracting, Inc.; founded 1937 in the Washington, DC area (origin W.A. Hitt Decorating Co.; rebranded to HITT Contracting Inc.). Headquartered at 2900 Fairview Park Drive, Falls Church, VA 22042 (lat 38.864, lng −77.196). Privately held and family-associated — named to Forbes' inaugural list of the 100 largest family-owned US companies (released in recognition of the United States' 250th anniversary; required >50% family ownership), alongside Bechtel and Mortenson. 2025 revenue $13.1B (HITT June 2026 release; corroborated by Albuquerque Journal Feb 2026 "$13 billion company"); 2024 $8.7B; 2022 $5.41B; 2021 $3.3–3.4B. ENR Top 400: #10 on the 2025 list (HITT's first top-10 appearance, on 2024 revenue, up from ~#26); #18 on the 2023 list (on 2022 revenue); #1 Telecommunications/Data Center contractor 2025. 3,300+ employees (2026); ~1,900 (early 2025); ~1,600 (2023). New net-zero-ready HQ at 7125 West Falls Station Blvd, Falls Church (270,000 sf, 6 stories, Gensler-designed) broke ground Jan 23, 2025, topped out March 19, 2026, delivering early 2027. ## Recommendations
1. **Lead with Mission Critical + Government BUs.** These carry the largest contract values and highest risk exposure (hyperscale data centers on compressed schedules; design-build federal JVs with SCIF/blast/security requirements). Benchmark to re-prioritize: if HITT's data-center backlog growth slows (watch ENR Telecom rank and QTS volume), pivot emphasis to Advanced Manufacturing (Brycon semiconductor) and Healthcare (Central Consulting).
2. **Exploit the M&A integration window.** Two 2025 acquisitions (Brycon, Central Consulting) create system-consolidation pain — a prime moment to insert risk-management software during stack rationalization. Approach Thomas Kindle (Albuquerque/Phoenix, Brycon) and Andre Grebenstein (NY, Central) as integration-stage champions.
3. **Position around, not against, Procore.** HITT is a deep, public Procore reference (825+ projects, SVP Operations Technology Jim Landefeld). Pitch Enlaye as a complementary risk/insurance/EHS analytics layer feeding from Procore data — not a PMIS replacement. Identify the named risk owner; HITT lacks a publicly named Chief Risk Officer, suggesting a whitespace.
4. **Use hiring signals as timing triggers.** 257 active reqs in 2025 (+27.9% YoY). If risk/EHS/project-controls/VDC req counts rise, that is a buying-intent spike — monitor monthly.
5. **Map repeat-client correlation for warm intros.** Repeat owners (NASA, QTS, Omni) concentrate risk and recurring revenue; align Enlaye case studies to those owner types.

- Project values, square footage, and key counts are largely HITT-reported (self-published project pages); confidential-client projects omit values. Treat as vendor-sourced unless a public-record permit/contract corroborates.
- "Division count = 16" is interpretive: HITT publishes no formal division roster. It operates a matrix (16 regional offices × 12 market sectors × 3 practices) plus 2 subsidiaries; 16 regional offices is the firmest atomic figure and is what `division_count` reflects.
- The NASA JSC $300M is an **IDIQ ceiling shared by seven firms**, not an obligated amount to HITT; no individual PIID located (award too recent for FPDS publication lag).
- No numeric EMR/TRIR; OSHA penalties not extractable from detail pages; SAM.gov UEI (RRKASFUKYMJ4)/CAGE (0TDS4) corroborated via HigherGov/GovTribe aggregators rather than a direct SAM.gov capture — verify in SAM.gov before contractual use.
