Kiewit Corporation — GC Intelligence Dossier (for Enlaye BD/Competitive Analysis)
TL;DR

Kiewit Corporation (Omaha, NE; founded 1884; employee-owned; $18.2B 2025 revenue; ~34,500 staff/craft; ENR #4 in 2026) operates through a decentralized network of ~14 major named operating subsidiaries organized along two axes — geographic districts (Central, Eastern, Underground, Northwest, Southern California, Southwest, Southeast, South Central) and market-sector units (Power, Power Delivery, Energy/OGC, Industrial/TIC, Building, Engineering, Mining, Marine/Weeks, Nuclear) — under the federal-contracting parent Peter Kiewit Sons', Inc.
The incumbent project-controls/risk software stack is InEight — a wholly-owned Kiewit subsidiary (rebranded from Hard Dollar, standardized 2011/acquired 2013) running on Microsoft Azure/Dynamics 365/Power BI, supplemented by 2025 AI tools KADE and ADAPT; Procore, Primavera P6, Bluebeam and Autodesk Construction Cloud appear in job reqs. This is the single most important competitive fact for Enlaye: Kiewit builds and sells its own risk/controls platform, so any third-party risk tool must displace or integrate with an in-house product the buyer profits from.
The active portfolio is dominated by mega-scale power/data-center (Homer City $10B EPC, NRG 5 GW), transportation (CA High-Speed Rail $3.5B, Key Bridge), water (Bull Run $2.56B program) and marine/federal (USACE Port Arthur $404M, Port of Nome $399.4M) work; USACE is the clearest repeat federal client.


Key Findings

Division count (real total): ~14 major operating subsidiaries, plus districts nested inside them and ~16 additional minor/specialty entities. There is no single "official" division count — Kiewit deliberately describes a "unique organizational structure" and "decentralized offices." The number depends on whether you count market-sector units, geographic districts, or legal subsidiaries. Eight markets are reported (Power 31%, Transportation 23%, Water 13%, Oil/Gas/Chemical 10%, Marine 9%, Building 7%, Industrial 6%, Mining 1% of 2024 revenue).
Parent/legal identity: The trade name is "Kiewit Corporation"; the SAM.gov-registered federal-contracting parent is Peter Kiewit Sons', Inc. (dba Kiewit), UEI M6G3M7N4XPS5 / CAGE 79AY2, primary NAICS 237990. Multiple subsidiaries hold their own UEI/CAGE.
Division→project mapping is explicit in most cases because Kiewit press releases and the USACE/owner records name the building subsidiary (e.g., "Kiewit Power Constructors Co." on Homer City; "Kiewit Infrastructure West Co." on Port of Nome; "Kiewit Infrastructure Co." on Key Bridge).
Newest awards (2025–2026): CA High-Speed Rail track & systems ($3.5B, awarded June 1 2026); Austin light-rail O&M facility (Apr 2026); Port of Nome ($399.4M, Aug 2025); Port Arthur PAV04 ($404M, May 2025); Homer City Energy Campus ($10B+, announced Apr 2025); Grain Belt Express ($1.7B w/ Quanta).
Notable risk event: Kiewit was off-ramped from Phase 2 of the Francis Scott Key Bridge rebuild (Apr 2026) after its Phase-2 proposal far exceeded the state's independent estimate — a live example of GMP/price-negotiation risk on a progressive design-build megaproject.
Compliance posture: 100% employee-owned/private (no SEC filings beyond legacy entities); Good Jobs First aggregates ~$6.86M in penalties / 71 records since 2000 for the Kiewit parent; a verified 2022 OSHA fatality inspection in San Jose carried a $27,500 penalty. No public EMR/TRIR number — Kiewit does not disclose them.

Recommendations (staged, with thresholds)
Stage 1 — Qualify the InEight problem (now). The decisive go/no-go question for Enlaye is whether Kiewit will buy an external risk tool when it owns InEight (which already sells "Basis" AI risk and Control/Document modules) and earns commercial revenue from it. Map exactly which risk workflows InEight does not cover well (e.g., enterprise insurance/wrap-up program management, subcontractor default/surety analytics, claims/dispute exposure modeling). Threshold to proceed: identify ≥2 named risk workflows InEight does not own and where Enlaye is demonstrably differentiated. If Enlaye overlaps InEight head-on, treat Kiewit as a low-probability, long-cycle target.
Stage 2 — Target the divisions scaling fastest and carrying the most external risk. Prioritize (a) Power/TIC/Kiewit Power Constructors (31% of revenue, Homer City $10B + NRG 5 GW + Grain Belt — huge schedule/price exposure on data-center energy) and (b) Marine/Weeks Marine (newly acquired 2023, still integrating systems — highest likelihood of a non-standardized stack). Threshold: if a target division still runs legacy Weeks/acquired systems rather than KMS/InEight, that is the wedge.
Stage 3 — Lead with the live pain points the record exposes. Use concrete, sourced events as the sales narrative: the Key Bridge Phase-2 off-ramp (a GMP/price-estimate breakdown on a PDB megaproject) and Bull Run's $0.5B→$2.56B cost escalation + 5-month land-use stop-work are textbook risk-quantification and change-exposure failures. Position Enlaye as the independent risk layer that owners increasingly demand on PDB/CM-GC megaprojects. Benchmark that would change the play: if Kiewit publicly adopts InEight Basis enterprise-wide for risk, pivot to an integration/owner-side play rather than displacement.
Stage 4 — Use the federal channel as a credibility wedge. USACE is a confirmed repeat client across ≥5 projects; federal/PLA jobs (Port Arthur, Nome, Homer City PLA) carry heavy compliance/Build America Buy America and prevailing-wage documentation burden. If Enlaye has compliance-documentation strength, enter via a single federal district (Infrastructure West, Vancouver WA — the busiest federal-award subsidiary). Threshold: a pilot on one USACE design-build job before any enterprise conversation.
