# Consigli Construction — BD Intelligence Dossier (Enlaye)

- Consigli is a ~$3.77B-revenue (2024), 100% employee-owned (ESOP) general contractor/CM headquartered in Milford, MA, ranked **#41 on ENR's 2025 Top 400 Contractors** (listed as "Consigli Building Group, Inc."), operating **15 regional offices** from Maine to the Caribbean plus a handful of market-sector/functional divisions (Riggs self-perform, Arch Energy, Interiors, Special Projects) — roughly **20 operating units total**.
- Projects map cleanly to **regional operating groups** (each led by a "Director of Operations" or regional President) and to **functional divisions** (Riggs for self-perform, Arch Energy for decarbonization, Consigli/Benton JV for the Caribbean); the 2024 acquisition of Lendlease's NY/NJ operations (400+ people, $1.8B backlog) created a large healthcare/life-sciences NY-Metro engine.
- Incumbent software stack is **Procore (PM) + Bluebeam + Autodesk BIM 360/VDC + Sage Estimating/On-Screen Takeoff/Togal + QuickBase + Revizto**, with an active **ERP migration from Sage 300 CRE to CMiC** — the CMiC cutover and risk/field gaps are the clearest wedge for a construction risk-management SaaS like Enlaye.

## Recommendations

1. **Lead with the CMiC transition.** Consigli is mid-ERP-migration — the ideal window to position a risk layer that complements (not competes with) CMiC and Procore. Benchmark to change the approach: if job postings stop referencing "transition to CMiC" (i.e., cutover complete), pivot messaging from "integrate during migration" to "augment the new system of record."
2. **Target the three highest-risk divisions first:** (a) **Advanced Technology & Mission Critical** (Dave Perez) running multi-GW confidential hyperscale data centers — schedule/commissioning risk is acute; (b) the **ex-Lendlease NY/NJ healthcare & life-sciences group** (Joseph Chiarelli / Laura Bush) with $1.8B of complex, regulated backlog; (c) large **public K-12/government** work where contract risk and surety exposure are high.
3. **Use the ESOP angle.** As a 100% employee-owned firm, risk-mitigation that protects margin directly benefits employee-owners — a resonant message for executive sponsors (CEO Anthony Consigli, President Matthew Consigli, EVP Jeff Navin).
4. **Engagement path:** Meet Consigli at AGC Massachusetts events (Jeff Navin is Vice-Chair) and the Women Builders Council circuit in NYC. If a pilot lands, expand division-by-division using the regional Director-of-Operations structure as the natural account-expansion map.
